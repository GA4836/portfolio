Dolma Recipe

Dolma is an ancient food, it goes as far back as the Ancient Assyrian Period (934BC-609BC).
It can have a large variety of ways to make it, with the main one being inside of grape leaves (which is what my recipe will be about).

It contains a mix of rice, spices, herbs, tomatoes, and more.

Directions

  1. 1. Boil your grape leaves for 2 minutes and let them dry in a strainer.
  2. 2. Cut the tomatoes, scallions, garlic, and parsley into very small pieces.
  3. 3. Mix the chopped items with your tomato sauce.
  4. 4. Add in your seasonings and mix.
  5. 5. Mix in your olive oil.
  6. 6. Wash your rice and strain it and then mix it with the mixture.
  7. 7. Clear a large space and prepare a large pot, your grape leaves, and your mixture.
  8. 8. Place one grape leaf on the table, then place mixture inside of it closing it similar to a burrito and put it inside of your pot. Repeat this until you have either no grape leaves or no mixture.
  9. 9. Place your pot on the stove and put a plate on top of your uncooked dolma, pour water until you see it just about touching the plate.
  10. 10. Turn your stove on high for 20 minutes and after this place it on low for 30 minutes.
  11. 11. Turn off the stove and let it cool.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds/1 jar of Grape Leaves
    • They must be wet/fermented(?)
    • Might have to purchase at a local store
  • 5 scallions
  • 3 tomatoes
  • 2 pieces of parsley
  • 15 ounces of tomato sauce
  • 4 cups of rice
  • 8 cloves of garlic
  • 2 spoons of salt
  • 1 spoon of black pepper
  • 2 spoons of citric acid
  • 1/2 spoon of red pepper
  • 1 cup olive oil
  • 1 1/2 cup of water
